Quarry · Art Bible

A living sacred painting of a ship.

Ōkami's ink-and-gold spirit worlds, crossed with Wind Waker's bright cel-shaded adventure, wrapped around a biomechanical vessel whose anatomy is the Kabbalistic Tree of Life. Nothing here is grey and grim — even horror is rendered in ink, bone, and gold, not gore.

This page is lit by the direction it describes — the two worlds are the two themes. Light is the physical world; dark is the World of Emanation. Cross over, top-right.

The thesis in four hands

The fusion

We are not copying either game — we borrow specific, nameable qualities, and let the biomech and the sacred make them one thing.

Ōkami

The ink & the bloom

  • Sumi-e brush outlines that taper and swell
  • Washi-paper grain over all
  • Powers as blossoming ink & gold, not lens flare
  • Limited, vivid palette + gold leaf
Pull: Celestial Brush strokes, Konohana bloom, gold-outlined divine beings.
Wind Waker

The charm & the read

  • Cel shading: flat bands + weighty outlines
  • Silhouette-first everything
  • Warm color even in danger
  • Toon lighting & big particle pop
Pull: the toon-outline look, expressive eyes, wind/particle feedback.
Biomechanical

The body of the ship

  • Sinew + chitin plating + veins of light
  • Grown, not built
  • Giger's architecture, de-gore'd into ink & bone
  • Awe, not disgust
Pull: ribbed organ-corridors and fused forms — beautiful, not sickening.
Kabbalah

The meaning & anatomy

  • Tree of Life: 10 sefirot, 22 paths
  • Merkavah — the Chariot, the ascent
  • Sacred geometry & glowing letter-runes
  • Sacred & accurate — never decoration
Treated with consultants; the ship's veins are the 22 paths made real.
Concept · the centerpiece

Plate IThe Cylinder

The idea that makes Quarry Quarry: the ship's power system is literally the Tree of Life. Ten glowing organ-chambers joined by 22 luminous veins that pulse with harvested life; a Merkavah star for a heart. Sacred geometry as anatomy — never wallpaper.

Two renders, one game

03The two worlds

The physical/spirit split is a render state, not just a mood — and it doubles as gameplay legibility. Pressing F should shift the whole frame toward the sacred. (That's the toggle you're holding: this page's light and dark are these worlds.)

Physical · Assiah
Grounded, warm, Wind Waker. Thick confident ink, fuller cel color, toon key light with warm bounce. The world of action.
Spirit · Atzilut
Ethereal, luminous, Ōkami. Outlines dissolve into brushstroke, color desaturates toward gold and pale blue, bloom and paper-grain rise, and hidden things (spirit-only paths, runes) reveal as glowing ink.
In engine
One toggled screen shader sells the ascent: desaturate, push gold tint, raise bloom + grain, thin the outlines.
Direction, made technical

04Render · VFX · UI

Cel & ink
Banded toon diffuse (2–3 bands) + screen-space ink outlines (depth+normal edge detect). A subtle washi grain multiplied over the whole frame so it reads painted.
Everything sacred glows
Veins, runes, sefirot, portals, spirit — all on emission, read through WorldEnvironment bloom. Author cel ramps, not photoreal PBR.
VFX language
Powers = Ōkami ink-bloom (the spirit blade is one calligraphic stroke). Feedback = Wind Waker pop (wind-lines, sparkle bursts). Sacred tech opens as a rotating Merkavah + Seed-of-Life ring.
UI
Parchment, ink, and gold-leaf frames with sacred-geometry corners. Diegetic where possible — the spirit meter is a sefirah filling with light. Calm and sacred, the opposite of a busy sci-fi HUD.
Guardrails

05Do & don't

✓ Do

  • Treat Kabbalah motifs as sacred, accurate, intentional — consult from pre-production.
  • Silhouette-test everything; keep the palette disciplined; keep gold earned.
  • Hold the ink-and-bloom beauty even in the ship's most harvested places.

✕ Don't

  • Photoreal gore — we're ink and bone, awe not disgust.
  • Hebrew or sacred symbols as random decoration or wallpaper.
  • Muddy "gritty sci-fi," or lose the silhouette to detail.
