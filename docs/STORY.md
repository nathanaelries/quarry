# Quarry — Story, Characters & World

> **Full spoilers.** This is the reference canon for narrative and worldbuilding.

---

## Logline

Abducted onto a living starship that harvests worlds, an Israeli-American mechanic awakens
the ancestral, mystical powers of her heritage to rescue the man she loves — and is forced
to choose whether to destroy the ship or become its god.

---

## Protagonist

**Abigail "Abby" Koval** — Israeli-American mechanic and former U.S. Army soldier living in
Miami (the Highland Lakes neighborhood). Pragmatic, dry, and at the start somewhat cynical
about her heritage. Competent with her hands and under fire; emotionally guarded. Her arc
is from *rejecting* her culture to *drawing power from it* — and from a purely personal
motivation (save Ben) toward a cosmic responsibility she never asked for.

## Supporting cast

| Character | Role |
|---|---|
| **Ben** | Abby's boyfriend; owns a jewelry shop in Aventura Mall. Tragic fate early-to-mid game (see beats). |
| **Elias Koval** | Abby's grandfather; a respected elder and her spiritual guide. Killed early; returns as a spirit who grants and teaches her powers. |
| **Aya** | Spirit-hawk guide/helper (the ghost of a childhood companion). Scouts, marks paths, offers guidance. *(Renamed from the brief's "Talon"; see DESIGN.md editorial notes.)* |
| **Eliana** | Leader of **The Remnant**, the human resistance inside the ship. Pragmatic commander; central to the sequel hook. |
| **The Mother** | Primary antagonist. A telepathic entity who was once human and overthrew the previous Mother to seize control of the Cylinder. Tragic, not cartoonish. |
| **The Stranger** | A mysterious cybernetic figure who frees Abby early and opposes the Cylinder. Loyalties and origin are a mid/late reveal. |

## The World

### The Cylinder
A colossal organic-technological starship — the game's entire world. It seeds life on
planets across the galaxy, then returns to **harvest** it, converting conquered species
into mindless **drones**. Interior features:

- Vast cavernous chambers.
- Floating **planetoids**, each with its own gravity.
- Districts of harvested races reduced to drone labor.
- Living, breathing, hostile regions — the ship reacts to intrusion.

The ship constantly **shifts, consumes, and adapts**. It is treated as a character, not a
backdrop.

### The Keepers
The ancient beings who created the Cylinder. It is their instrument: seed, return, harvest,
repeat. Their nature and current whereabouts are a thread left partly open (sequel fuel).

### The Remnant
A resistance of humans (and possibly other freed species) surviving and fighting from
within the ship, led by **Eliana**. They are Abby's allies and her connection to a larger
war.

### The Mother
Whoever pilots the Cylinder's will. The role is a throne: the current Mother was human and
took the seat by overthrowing her predecessor — which frames the climax choice as a
recurring, cyclical temptation rather than a one-off.

## The mystical frame

Abby's powers are drawn from **Kabbalah** and **Merkavah mysticism**:

- **The Four Worlds.** In her near-death crossing she enters **Atzilut, the World of
  Emanation** — the highest and most sublime of the four (Atzilut / Beriah / Yetzirah /
  Assiyah). It is where Elias's spirit meets her and where powers are granted and deepened.
- **Merkavah ("the Chariot").** The tradition of the mystic's ascent — the "Work of the
  Chariot," rooted in Ezekiel's vision and the Hekhalot ("palaces") ascent literature. In
  Quarry this is literalized as **spirit projection**: the mystic leaves the body to walk
  higher planes (here, walls, ceilings, and hidden truths).
- **The Sefirot.** The ten emanations of the Tree of Life structure Abby's **progression**
  (see DESIGN.md §5.3).

Handled respectfully as mythic structure — see DESIGN.md §9.

## Story beats (canonical order)

1. **The shop.** The game opens in Ben's jewelry shop in Aventura Mall. A robbery is
   underway when the *entire building* is lifted into the sky by a green tractor beam.
2. **Abduction.** Abby, Ben, Elias, and others are taken onto the Cylinder.
3. **Freed.** The cybernetic **Stranger** frees Abby from processing.
4. **Elias's death.** She watches, unable to stop it, as Elias is killed in an alien
   machine.
5. **The crossing.** In a near-death experience she enters the **World of Emanation**.
   Elias's spirit grants her ancestral powers and charges her to protect Earth. Her own
   goal stays personal: **find Ben**.
6. **Ben.** She finds Ben surgically fused to a grotesque reptile-like host and is forced
   to **mercy-kill** him — the game's emotional turn.
7. **The truth.** She learns the Cylinder is an organic vessel of the **Keepers**: seed,
   return, harvest; conquered species become drones.
8. **The Remnant.** She links up with **Eliana**'s resistance and fights from within.
9. **The Mother.** She confronts The Mother — once human, now the ship's telepathic will.
10. **The choice.** Become the new Mother (god-like control of the Cylinder) or destroy it.
11. **The descent.** Canonically, Abby pilots the Cylinder **into the sun**. She dies —
    and **resurrects on Earth** months later.
12. **Hook.** The ending sets up a sequel involving **Eliana** and "powerful beings"
    (Keepers? something worse?).

## Threads left open (sequel fuel)

- Who/what the **Keepers** are, and whether the destroyed Cylinder was one of many.
- **Eliana** and the Remnant after the ship's loss.
- The nature of Abby's **resurrection** on Earth — and what came back with her.
- The "powerful beings" named in the ending.
